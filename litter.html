The Consequences of Littering

Littering is a big issue in our world, and in New Zealand. Lots of our litter ends up in the oceans, and some of that is eaten by marine life. Plastic is not good for marine life, as it logs up their insides, and can even kill them. The leatherback sea turtle think that plastic bags are jellyfish, their food, so eat it. Some fish eat microplastics because they look like caviar, so eat the plastic.

Did you know that the Great Pacific garbage patch has over 1 trillion pieces of rubbish in it, and weight 80,000 tons. The area it covers is 5x larger than the land area of New Zealand. It just takes people to throw something out of the car window, or eat something and just leave it in the park, and that is another unnecessary piece of rubbish where it does not belong. There are a few very simple ways to help the probelm of littering.

1. Don't Litter

The simplest way to solve this problem is but your rubbish in the bin. It is a really simple and easy way to solve the problem

2. Pick up Other's Litter

Picking up other litter around your neighbourhood is another way to stop litter travelling into the ocean, and into nature.

3. Tell Others about the Consequences of Littering!

Spreading the word around about littering can create an impact.
