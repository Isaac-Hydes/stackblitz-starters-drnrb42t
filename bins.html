The Bins around our School

Around our school, there are four different types of bin. There is the food scraps bin, reycling bin, soft plastics bin, and the rubbish bin. Each of these bins have different things that go in them, and this page will help you identify what goes in each bin. The main places where bins are around our school is outside the hall and outside the intermediate block.

Food Scraps

The food scraps bin has organic food going into it, such as fruit and vegetables. Things that can not go in the food scraps bin are processed foods, such as sandwiches.

Recycling

The recycling binis where materials that can be reused go, such as paper/cardboard, glass, tins/cans, and SOME plastics. These are plastics 1, 2 and 5. These plastics are things such as plastic bottles and sushi containers. Plastcs 1, 2 and 5 are considered 'hard plastics'. Only put things in the reycling bin if they are clean. If it is dirty, clean it or put it in the rubbish bin. Thinks that go in the recycling bin also need to be bigger than 5cm.

Soft Plastics

Soft Plastics are plastics that are 'soft'. These are things that would crinkle in your hand, like chip packets, muesli bar wrappers, and cling film.

Rubbish

Everything else that does not fall into a category goes into the rubbish bin. This is things like juice boxes, milk lids, and dirty containers.

A useful saying is:

'If in doubt, chuck it out'

If you are unsure what bin something goes, put it in the rubbish bin. All the other bins have people sort through them, and it uses their time to sort through it all. If you put something in the wrong bin, they have to sort it out.
